Architecture Dictionary

How the DHL eCommerce BI Costing Framework Works

This framework provides a standardized methodology to capture, align, and benchmark the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for Business Intelligence across the global network. It transforms raw IT spending into actionable business metrics like Cost per Shipment.

The 6-Step Data Pipeline

Click on each architectural step below to understand how to locate and split its costs from your Cloud Bill.

A. Integration (ETL / Event Hubs)

Definition: The process and compute required to move data from operational systems (like ERPs, CRMs, or external APIs) into the analytics environment.

How to Identify in your Bill: Look for services like Azure Data Factory, Azure Event Hubs, GCP Dataflow, AWS Glue, Talend, or Informatica Cloud. Only include the compute costs associated with data movement and ingestion pipelines.

👨‍💻 FTE / Human Capital Mapping: Include salaries for **Data Engineers, Data Integrators, and ETL Developers** here.

B. Storage (Data Lakes / Warehouses)

Definition: The physical or logical environments where raw, curated, and modeled data rests natively.

How to Identify in your Bill: The baseline storage tiers of Azure Data Lake (ADLS Gen2), Amazon S3, Google Cloud Storage, or Snowflake. Note: This is purely storage capacity (Terabytes per month), separate from the compute used to query it.

👨‍💻 FTE / Human Capital Mapping: Include salaries for **Database Administrators (DBAs) and Storage/Infrastructure Engineers** here.

C. Processing (Compute / SQL Engines)

Definition: The compute power required to execute queries, transform data (ETL/ELT), and train machine learning models.

How to Identify in your Bill: Look for heavy compute resources like Databricks, Azure Synapse dedicated SQL pools (Compute tier), Google BigQuery (Analysis/Compute tier), Snowflake (Warehouses), or Spark clusters.

👨‍💻 FTE / Human Capital Mapping: Include salaries for heavy compute developers: **Analytics Engineers (dbt experts) and Data Scientists** training models here.

D. Analytics & Consumption (BI Tools)

Definition: Frontend BI platforms, ad-hoc query tools, or embedded portals where users actually consume the insights.

How to Identify in your Bill: Software licensing and premium capacities for PowerBI Premium/Pro, Tableau Cloud, Qlik, Looker, or custom frontend web app hosting.

👨‍💻 FTE / Human Capital Mapping: Include salaries strictly for the frontend builders: **BI Developers, PowerBI Dashboarders, and Data Analysts**.

E. Governance (Security & Cataloging)

Definition: Enforcing data security, managing access control, and operating data catalogs/lineage tools.

How to Identify in your Bill: Identity management (Azure AD B2C for analytics portals), Azure Purview, Collibra, Alation, or specific Secrets/Key Vault usage tied directly to the BI team.

👨‍💻 FTE / Human Capital Mapping: Include overarching salaries for **Data Architects, Data Stewards, and Compliance Officers** here.

F. Operations (Orchestration & DevOps)

Definition: Triggering workflows, monitoring pipeline health, and deploying code.

How to Identify in your Bill: Apache Airflow, Azure DevOps runners, GitHub Actions (billed minutes), Azure Monitor / Log Analytics environments strictly related to the BI resource group.

👨‍💻 FTE / Human Capital Mapping: Include overarching operational salaries for **DevOps Engineers, Platform Admins, Scrum Masters, and L1/L2 Support**.

Data Quality & Comparability Rubric

Understanding how the Platform calculates the /5 Confidence Score for each Country profile.

+1 Point if the Country has provided reliable target Shipments (the core denominator for Cost per Shipment standardisation).
+1 Point if the Country has provided Active BI Application Users (crucial for evaluating pure application scaling costs).
+1 Point if costs are mapped specifically to architectural steps rather than dumped as a single aggregate number. Missing 3 or more structural layers fails this check.
+1 Point if the Country has entered trend data for at least 6 months inside the 2025/2026 Grid, enabling forecasting analysis.
+1 Point if advanced driver inputs (Queries, Revenue, TB Size) are consistently tracked.
Core KPI Definitions & Optimization Levers
The BI Costing Performance Dashboard tracks standardized metrics to compare countries impartially. Below is the operational dictionary outlining the significance and improvement strategy for each KPI.
Definition: The total BI software, cloud, and FTE cost divided by the total number of physical DHL eCommerce shipments delivered in that country.
Why It Matters: This is the ultimate equalizer. A massive country with a huge BI bill might actually be highly efficient on a per-shipment basis compared to a small country with a tiny BI bill.
Optimization Levers: Eliminate redundant cloud storage, deprecate unused legacy reports, or increase shipment volume without scaling infrastructure linearly.
Definition: Total BI cost divided by the number of active employees utilizing the BI dashboards.
Why It Matters: Reveals the licensing and compute cost of democratizing data. Extremely high cost-per-user indicates severe inefficiencies or very expensive bespoke tooling for a small audience.
Optimization Levers: Audit and revoke inactive software licenses, shift from named-user to capacity-based pricing, or train more staff to utilize the existing platform (increasing the denominator).
Definition: The total Terabytes (TB) of data housed in the cloud divided by total shipments.
Why It Matters: Indicates data bloat. Tracking too many irrelevant event pings per shipment explodes storage costs without adding operational value.
Optimization Levers: Implement aggressive data retention policies, move cold data to cheaper archive storage classes, and stop duplicating tables across multiple reporting layers.
Definition: The percentage of the country's entire IT budget consumed specifically by Business Intelligence.
Why It Matters: Highlights the strategic footprint of data analytics within the local IT org.
Optimization Levers: If this ratio is unsustainably high, local architects need to renegotiate vendor contracts or migrate away from fully-managed premium SaaS toward open-source self-managed tools.
Definition: The percentage variance in Total Cost comparing Month 24 against Month 1.
Why It Matters: Cloud costs inherently creep upwards. A steep positive % means costs are spiraling out of control faster than the business is growing.
Optimization Levers: Implement strict FinOps alerting, mandate query-optimization standards for developers, and utilize auto-pausing/auto-scaling cloud warehouse clusters.
